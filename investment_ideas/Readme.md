## 1. Core Philosophy

Indian investors often underperform because they:
- Chase recent market leaders
- Concentrate heavily in popular sectors
- Confuse short-term momentum with long-term safety

Contrarian investing in India is not about opposing the market â€”

## 3. The 3-P Test (India Context)

Before investing, classify the idea:

### Possible
- A new manufacturing cycle boosting Indian exports

### Plausible
- PSU banks improving profitability due to balance sheet cleanup

### Probable
- Valuation multiples reverting toward historical averages

## 4. Common India-Specific Contrarian Opportunities (Historically)

- Buying equities after major corrections  
- Investing in value or dividend strategies after growth-led rallies  
- Maintaining gold allocation during equity bull markets  
- Holding international exposure despite domestic optimism
